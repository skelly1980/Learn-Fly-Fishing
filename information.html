What you'll need to get started

Required Gear

Listen to the folks from Mad River about what type of gear you'll need to get started in fly fishing. Brian makes it very simple by breaking things down into a list of six basic things that you need to get started in fly fishing. You can head over to Mad River Outfitters youTube page and view the playlist they created on all six of these listed items. My list of items is almost exactly the same as the list Brain created. You'll need a fly rod, reel, fly line system, some flies, and finally something to hold all of your gear. The common misconception that fly fishing is an expensive hobby is not true. You can get all these items at a budget of somewhere's around $200-$300 dollars. The one thing Brain does not mention is a fishing license. Make sure to follow your local rules and regulations and purchase a fishing license if it is required.

Suggestions on how to progress

After learning the basics on how to cast and set up you gear, I generally discourage people from immediately heading out to your nearest trout stream which is what most people want to do. Trout fishing is generally more difficult. It requires waders which is another large investment that one might not want to spend just yet. I recommend finding a local pond first and chasing some easier, more plentiful fish. This ites in with how to locate spots but catching a bunch of sunfish or bluegills will be a better experience for beginners before heading out to your local trout streams.

How to find places close to home

When attempting to find local fishing spots the first thing I do is look for town parks or wildlife management areas. Such places can be excellent for beginners as they are usually well maintained. The water is usually clear of trees which leaves plenty of room to back cast with worrying about getting hung up. This also means that waders are not required since you wouldn't have to get in the water. Places like these are usually full of sunfish, bluegill, and largemouth bass. Each of these three species will readily eat flies. Catching 20-30 fish in an hour is not an unresonable expectation. Should you be teaching a child I highly recommend such places as it will keep them very busy.
