Here is the most important part of the equation of a fly rod and reel outfit. The fly rod is like the engine on a car. It's what makes the car move. If budget allows this is where the bulk of the investment should go. A nicer rod will have the most impact on your skill level than any of the other components. The danger in buying a cheaper flyrod is out growing it quickly and having to replace it. Thats why I don't recommend prepackaged outfits. All of the rods I have suggested above would never need to be replaced due to them being out grown.

This is perhaps the most intimidating part of fly fishing for most beginners. Understanding what flies to use and how to match the correct tippet to a leader. What leaders to use under what circumstances and how to know when to add more tippet on to a leader. I have a few simple suggestions as to how to proceed while learning these things. First, when most people begin fly fishing the first rod they purchase is usually a 5-weight either 9ft or 8'6". The easiest way to match fly lines,leaders, and tippet is the rule of 5's. A 5-weight rod gets a 5-weight fly line, a 9ft 5x leader, and the leader gets 5x tippet. That is a good place to start and avoids a lot of the cofusion. As far as how to know when to add more tippet to your leader there is a very simple trick. When you get a new leader snip off eight inches from the thin end or the end without the loop. Pull of eight inches of tippet and re-attach it to your leader. Doing it this way will make it so every time you get near that knot you know it's time to add eight more inches of tippet on. The above photos are suggestions of the type of leaders, tippet, and flies that most beginners start with.
