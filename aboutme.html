About Me

Shawn Kelly

Shawn began fly fishing at the age of eight after watching two anglers catching Striped Bass of a beach in Long Island Sound. He immediately bought a rod and reel and began chasing Stripers on the fly. His passion for the sport grew immensely and soon he began chasing everything he could with a fly rod. He caught his first False Albacore at the age of sixteen and since that day he looks forward to the fall run and getting out after these Atlantic speedsters. At the age of fourteen he was introduced to trout fishing the Delaware River system and it has become his favorite fishery. He has a passion for nymphing the trout that make these waters so famous. He has developed a passion for euro-nymphing over the past five years after spending time fishing the Neversink River which is a perfect river for this technique. After seeing how effective this technique was he began to study it further and employ it on many other rivers as well. When not fishing Shawn enjoys spending time at the vise tying up some of his favorite euro style, indicator nymphs, and saltwater patterns. Shawn also has a passion for drawing and painting as well. Many of his artworks are recreations of scenes he's viewed while out on the water. He also enjoys hunting deer and turkey every spring and fall as well.

We love to answer questions!

Questions, comments, let us know. The world of fly fishing may seem daunting but I'd like to make it much easier for everyone. The more new people we introduce to this sport the brighter it's future becomes. Please don't hesitate, there is no such thing as a bad question. Learning how to fly fish is a lot like building a house. You lay a foundation down and then add layers on top as you build. None of us so called experts learned it in one day and we are all still learning.
